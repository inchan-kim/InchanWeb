Inchan Kim

Associate Professor of Decision Sciences

Peter T. Paul College of Business and Economics
University of New Hampshire

I study how digital technologies shape organizations, markets, and everyday life. My work looks at how people talk about technology, how companies build and govern digital platforms, and how issues like responsibility, diversity, and fairness show up in the digital world.

My research has appeared in journals such as MIS Quarterly, Information & Management, International Journal of Information Management, Journal of Business Research, and Internet Research, and at conferences including ICIS and the Academy of Management.

In the classroom, I work with students at the intersection of business, analytics, and technology. I especially enjoy helping students see how technical skills and human stories come together in data-driven decision making.

What I Work On

My projects sit at the crossroads of digital technology, business strategy, and social impact. A few recurring themes:

  • How people make sense of new digital technologies and how that shapes what gets built and adopted.
  • How digital platforms and app ecosystems grow, change, and affect entrepreneurs.
  • How corporate social responsibility and diversity efforts connect to financial markets.
  • How AI and advanced analytics can help us understand complex digital phenomena.

Teaching Snapshot

I teach courses that connect business fundamentals with analytics, digital technology, and entrepreneurship. Representative courses include:

  • Management Information Systems – how organizations use technology and data.
  • Technology & Competitive Edge – how firms create advantage using digital tools.
  • Startup Analytics – using data to build and scale entrepreneurial ventures.
  • E-Business – digital business models and platforms.
